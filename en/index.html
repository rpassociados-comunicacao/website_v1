Activity

Administrative Litigation refers to the set of legal proceedings involving the Public Administration and its acts. This branch of law aims to ensure that administrative acts, such as decisions, rules or contracts, are carried out in accordance with the law, protecting citizens' rights against possible abuses of power by the state. Through administrative litigation, individuals can question the legality of administrative acts and seek compensation for damages caused by the actions of public authorities.

This type of litigation involves analyzing administrative decisions that directly affect citizens' interests, such as tax issues, licenses or permits. When a person or company considers that their rights have been harmed by an administrative act, they can go to the administrative court to seek a review or annulment of that decision. Administrative litigation is therefore a crucial tool for ensuring transparency, legality and justice in the actions of the state.

Tax Litigation deals with legal disputes between taxpayers and the state relating to the application of tax laws. This branch of law involves resolving conflicts over the obligation to pay taxes, interpreting tax rules, and challenging tax assessments and penalties imposed by tax authorities. The focus is on ensuring that taxation is fair and that tax obligations are met in accordance with current legislation.

This field includes defending the rights of taxpayers in situations of undue collection, challenging taxes and other fiscal measures, as well as regulating the actions of the state in supervising and collecting taxes. The aim is to ensure a balance between compliance with tax obligations and the protection of taxpayers' rights under the tax system.

Commercial and Corporate Law regulates economic and business activities, ensuring a stable legal framework for the creation, management and dissolution of companies. This branch of law covers the rules governing relations between traders, companies and other economic agents, promoting security and transparency in the market.

Fundamental to the functioning of companies, Commercial and Corporate Law defines the rules for setting up companies, commercial contracts, the liability of partners and directors, as well as the mechanisms for resolving business disputes. Its application aims to ensure balance in commercial relations and foster a solid and competitive business environment.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Commercial contracts

Family Law is the branch of law that regulates legal relations between members of a family, dealing with essential issues such as marriage, de facto unions, filiation, custody of children, adoption and succession. This field of law aims to protect the individual and collective rights of family members, promoting harmonious coexistence and the well-being of the people involved. Legislation in this area also deals with the protection of children, the elderly and other vulnerable family members.

In addition, Family Law deals with issues such as the property regime in marriage, the sharing of assets, alimony and the definition of parental responsibilities. The aim is to resolve family disputes in a balanced and fair manner, ensuring that the rights of each individual are respected and that the best interests of children are always a priority in judicial decisions.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Paternity and maintenance claims
  • Actions for Separation of Property and of Persons and Property
  • Adoption
  • Catholic marriage annulment
  • Disinheritance
  • Divorce by mutual consent and without consent of other spouse
  • Family mediation
  • Protection of Children and Young People at Risk
  • Parental Responsibilities

Inheritance/Inventory Law deals with the transfer of a deceased person's assets, rights and duties to their heirs. This branch of law covers the rules relating to testamentary and legitimate succession, i.e. the distribution of assets in accordance with what was established in the will or, in the absence of a will, in accordance with legal provisions. Succession law also deals with the liability of heirs in relation to the deceased's debts and the management of the estate.

Inventory is a legal procedure that aims to identify and evaluate the assets left by the deceased, as well as determine the rights and obligations of the heirs. This process is essential for formalizing the division of assets and can be carried out judicially or extrajudicially, depending on the complexity of the case and the agreement of the heirs. Through probate, the aim is to ensure that the succession is carried out fairly and in accordance with current legislation, avoiding conflicts between those involved.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Judicial and Extrajudicial Sharing of Assets
  • Wills, exchanges and donations

Consumer Law is a branch of law that protects the interests of consumers in their relations with suppliers of goods and services. This field covers various issues, such as defense against unfair commercial practices, misleading advertising and unfair contract terms. The main objective is to ensure that consumers have access to clear information, that their rights are respected and that they can seek redress for damages suffered as a result of illegal or harmful practices.

In the legal context, consumer law seeks to balance the relationship between consumers and companies, recognizing the vulnerability of the consumer and ensuring that they are protected from practices that could harm their physical, moral or patrimonial integrity. This includes the right to return products, repair damages and compensation for losses suffered, always based on the legislation that regulates consumer relations.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Warranties

Civil Law is the basis of the legal system and regulates relations between private individuals, ensuring rights and duties in everyday society. This branch of law covers several areas, including contractual obligations, civil liability, rights in rem, family relations and inheritance.

Essential for guaranteeing justice in interactions between individuals, Civil Law establishes the principles governing contracts, possession and ownership of property, as well as rights and duties in family relationships. Its application aims to promote legal certainty, protect citizens' interests and ensure the fair resolution of private conflicts.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Civil lawsuits
  • Family and minors
  • Obligations and contracts
  • Horizontal property
  • Succession and Wills
  • Judicial courts

Labor Law and Litigation regulates labor relations between employers and employees, ensuring a balance between the rights and duties of both parties. This area of law covers the conclusion, execution and termination of employment contracts, the protection of workers' rights, as well as the regulation of working conditions, safety and health at work.

Labor litigation is the resolution of conflicts arising from employment relationships, whether by negotiation, administrative or judicial means. Issues such as dismissals, harassment at work, accidents at work, non-compliance with contractual rules and wage claims are often the subject of disputes that require specialized legal intervention to guarantee the defence of the interests of those involved.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Contracts
  • Employment relationship

Criminal Law and Litigation is the branch of law that regulates criminal offenses and the sanctions applicable to individuals who commit crimes. This field of law defines what constitutes a crime, the procedures for investigation, prosecution and trial, and the penalties that can be imposed on offenders. Criminal Litigation refers specifically to legal disputes related to crimes, where the state, through the Public Prosecutor's Office, prosecutes individuals who break the law.

The aim of criminal law is to ensure social order by preventing behavior that could harm the community, punishing those responsible and rehabilitating offenders whenever possible. In criminal proceedings, the defendant has constitutional rights to a fair and impartial trial, and the defense lawyer plays an essential role in protecting these rights, being able to challenge the evidence, question the allegations and plead for acquittal or reduced sentences.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Administrative offenses
  • Criminal law

Executive Law is the area of law that regulates procedures for the coercive collection of obligations, such as debts, the fulfillment of contracts and the enforcement of court decisions. Through the legal means available, it aims to ensure that creditors obtain satisfaction of their claims when debtors do not voluntarily fulfill their obligations.

This branch of law covers mechanisms such as seizures, garnishments, foreclosures and other coercive measures, ensuring that creditors can recover the amounts owed in a fair and effective manner, always respecting the fundamental rights of the parties involved.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Debt collection letters
  • Initiation of enforcement proceedings
  • Injunctive proceedings
  • Others

Real Estate Law is the branch of law that regulates legal relationships relating to real estate, including the purchase, sale, rental, construction, use and administration of real estate. This field covers both property rights and rights in rem over real estate, as well as issues related to possession and use of real estate. It also deals with the drafting and execution of real estate contracts, such as purchase and sale agreements, lease agreements, exchange agreements, among others.

In addition, Real Estate Law deals with matters such as regularizing properties, obtaining licenses and permits, and resolving disputes arising from land issues. This branch also involves analyzing contracts and resolving conflicts between buyers, sellers, lessors and lessees, always with the aim of ensuring legal certainty and compliance with the legal rules applicable to the real estate sector.

Road Traffic Law and Litigation regulates traffic rules and road safety, as well as the resolution of disputes arising from traffic violations and accidents. This branch of law aims to guarantee the correct application of traffic rules, protect the rights of drivers, pedestrians and other road users, and ensure civil and criminal liability in cases of infringement.

It also covers the defense of drivers in administrative and judicial proceedings, challenging administrative offenses, liability actions for damages arising from road accidents and issues related to motor insurance. Its aim is to promote safety on public roads and ensure the fair resolution of conflicts in the road sector.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Driving license
  • Car registration
  • Transit Appeals against administrative offenses

Notaries and Registries is an area of law that ensures the authenticity, validity and publicity of legal acts, guaranteeing their legality and legal certainty. Notaries play an essential role in formalizing contracts, deeds, wills and other acts that require public faith.

In turn, the registration system ensures the registration and publicity of relevant legal facts, such as civil registration (births, marriages and deaths), land registration (transfers and charges on real estate) and commercial registration (incorporation and changes to companies). This area of law is fundamental to guaranteeing the transparency, security and legal effectiveness of property and business relations.

Within this area we work on the following issues:

  • Document authentication
  • Residence permit
  • Nationality
  • Signature recognition
  • Registries
  • Company formation/dissolution
